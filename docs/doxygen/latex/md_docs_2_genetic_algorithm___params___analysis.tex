Population size determines the number of individuals in each generation. A larger population size increases the genetic diversity but also requires more computational resources.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md85}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Analysis Results}{Analysis Results}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md85}



\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Population sizes of 600, 800, and 1000 start with relatively high initial fitness values, indicating that they quickly find high-\/quality solutions.
\item The fitness values increase rapidly in the early generations.
\item A population size of 400 shows the fastest increase in fitness, reaching 167.\+378 by generation 20.
\item All population sizes eventually stabilize at a fitness value of 167.\+378, demonstrating effective convergence of the algorithm.
\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md86}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Parameter Choice}{Parameter Choice}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md86}
A population size of 400 is optimal as it balances computational cost and convergence speed effectively.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md87}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Tournament Size}{Tournament Size}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md87}
Tournament size determines the number of individuals randomly selected from the population for each tournament. The winner of each tournament, being the individual with the highest fitness, is selected to be a parent for the next generation.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md88}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Analysis Results}{Analysis Results}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md88}



\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item All tournament sizes show a rapid increase in fitness within the first 10 generations.
\item Tournament Size 5 reaches the highest fitness value (around 165) by generation 20 and remains stable.
\item Sizes 6 and 7 also achieve high fitness values but are slightly lower than Tournament Size 5.
\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md89}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Parameter Choice}{Parameter Choice}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md89}
Choosing a tournament size of 5 ensures that the genetic algorithm efficiently explores the solution space and quickly converges to high-\/quality solutions, making it suitable for most optimization problems.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md90}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Crossover Rate}{Crossover Rate}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md90}
The crossover rate is a probability value (usually between 0 and 1) that dictates the likelihood of applying the crossover operation to generate new offspring from two parent individuals. It helps control the genetic diversity in the population by mixing genes from different parents.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md91}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Analysis Results}{Analysis Results}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md91}



\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Crossover rates of 0.\+7 and 0.\+8 demonstrate the fastest convergence, achieving the maximum fitness value the quickest.
\item All crossover rates eventually reach the same maximum fitness value, indicating robustness across different rates.
\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md92}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Parameter Choice}{Parameter Choice}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md92}
For future runs, consider using a crossover rate of 0.\+8 to achieve the fastest improvement and robust performance.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md93}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Mutation Rate}{Mutation Rate}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md93}
Mutation rate is a crucial parameter in genetic algorithms, representing the probability of randomly altering genes in an individual\textquotesingle{}s genome. It helps maintain genetic diversity within the population, preventing premature convergence to local optima and enabling the exploration of the solution space.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md94}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Analysis Results}{Analysis Results}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md94}



\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Lower mutation rates (0.\+01, 0.\+05, and 0.\+1) demonstrate quicker convergence and higher final fitness values compared to higher mutation rates (0.\+2 and 0.\+3).
\item Mutation rate 0.\+01 shows the fastest and most stable improvement.
\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md95}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Parameter Choice}{Parameter Choice}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md95}
Use a mutation rate of 0.\+01 to achieve rapid convergence and robust performance.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md96}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Elite Percentage}{Elite Percentage}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md96}
The elite\+Percentage parameter in a genetic algorithm controls the elitism strategy. This strategy directly preserves the best-\/performing individuals from the current generation into the next generation without any mutation or crossover. This ensures that the top-\/quality genes are passed on, enhancing the algorithm\textquotesingle{}s efficiency and stability.\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md97}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Analysis Results}{Analysis Results}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md97}



\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item elite\+Percentage values of 0.\+01 show the highest fitness values consistently, reaching a fitness of 836.\+835 by generation 250.
\item elite\+Percentage values of 0.\+05, 0.\+1, and 0.\+2 show slower fitness increases, with all stabilizing around 829.\+175 by generation 250.
\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md98}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Parameter Choice}{Parameter Choice}}\label{md_docs_2_genetic_algorithm___params___analysis_autotoc_md98}
